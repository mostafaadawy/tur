  1. Set up your scene: Create or import your 3D objects and set up your desired camera angles, lighting, and materials.

  2. Set the frame range: Specify the range of frames you want to render. Go to the "Time Configuration" dialog by clicking "Customize" > "Preferences" > "Animation." In the "Time Configuration" dialog, set the "Frame Range" according to your requirements.

  3. Adjust render settings: Open the "Render Setup" dialog by pressing F10 or going to "Rendering" > "Render Setup." In the "Common" tab, choose the desired render output format, such as PNG or JPEG, from the "File" group.   4. Enable rendering as separate frames: In the "Common" tab of the "Render Setup" dialog, ensure that the "Render Output" group is expanded. Check the box next to "Separate Files" to enable rendering of individual frames as separate images.

  5. Choose output location: Specify the output directory where the rendered frames will be saved. Click on the "Files" button in the "Render Output" group and choose the desired folder.

  6. Start the rendering process: Once you have set up the render settings, click on the "Render" button to start the rendering process. 3ds Max will render each frame individually and save them as separate images in the specified output directory.

By following these steps, you can render your video as frames/images with high resolution in 3ds Max 2016.

  3. Apply LoopOut: Right-click on the selected keyframes and choose "LoopOut" from the context menu. This action will create a looping motion that maintains a uniform speed throughout each repetition.

  4. Adjust the animation length: If needed, adjust the length of your animation to accommodate the number of repetitions you want. You can extend or reduce the frame range in the "Time Configuration" dialog by going to "Customize" > "Preferences" > "Animation."
