All three phrases – "my journey began," "my journey commenced," and "my journey embarked" – are acceptable and convey a similar meaning. However, each has a slightly different nuance:

  1. "My journey began": This is a straightforward and commonly used phrase. It implies the starting point of your journey without any particular emphasis on the process of starting.

  2. "My journey commenced": This phrase carries a slightly formal tone and indicates a deliberate or planned start. It suggests that your journey was initiated purposefully.

  3. "My journey embarked": This phrase is more poetic and evocative. "Embarked" suggests a departure on a journey, often with a sense of adventure or exploration.

Ultimately, the choice among these phrases depends on the tone and style you want to convey. If you prefer a simple and direct statement, "My journey began" is a good choice. If you're aiming for a more formal or deliberate tone, "My journey commenced" works well. If you want a touch of literary flair, "My journey embarked" might be the preferred option.
